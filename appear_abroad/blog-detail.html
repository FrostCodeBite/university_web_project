Discovering Malta: A Perfect 3-Day Getaway

Friday , 6 Dec 2024

Malta, a Mediterranean gem, is the ideal destination for a short yet unforgettable escape. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture, this tiny island nation offers a blend of relaxation and adventure. Here’s how to make the most of three days in Malta.

Day 1: Valletta and the Three Cities

Start your journey in Valletta, Malta’s capital and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore St. John’s Co-Cathedral, known for its baroque architecture and Caravaggio masterpieces. Wander through the Upper Barrakka Gardens for panoramic views of the Grand Harbour. After lunch, take a ferry to the Three Cities—Vittoriosa, Senglea, and Cospicua. These charming, historic towns are perfect for a relaxed stroll along quiet streets and picturesque marinas. End your day dining at a waterfront restaurant, savoring Maltese specialties like rabbit stew or pastizzi.

The Citadel, also known as the Castello, is the citadel of Victoria on the island of Gozo, Malta.
Comino is a small island of the Maltese archipelago between the islands of Malta and Gozo in the Mediterranean Sea.

Day 2: Gozo and Comino Adventure

On your second day, venture to Gozo, Malta’s smaller sister island. Visit the stunning Ġgantija Temples, older than the pyramids of Egypt. Don’t miss the azure waters of Ramla Bay for a refreshing swim. In the afternoon, take a boat to Comino and marvel at the Blue Lagoon’s crystal-clear waters. Whether you swim, snorkel, or relax on the shore, this spot is sure to leave you in awe. Return to Malta and enjoy a laid-back evening.

Mdina, also known by its Italian epithets Città Vecchia and Città Notabile, is a fortified city in the Northern Region of Malta which served as the island's former capital.

Day 3: Mdina and Golden Bay

Spend your final day exploring Mdina, the “Silent City.” This ancient walled city boasts narrow, winding streets and breathtaking views from its bastions. Stop for coffee and cake at Fontanella Tea Garden, a local favorite. In the afternoon, head to Golden Bay, one of Malta’s most beautiful beaches. Unwind on the golden sands, take a dip in the sea, or catch a spectacular sunset to conclude your trip.

Final Thoughts

Malta may be small, but it’s packed with experiences that cater to history buffs, adventure seekers, and beach lovers alike. A three-day trip is just enough to savor its charm while leaving you eager to return for more. Whether you’re exploring ancient ruins, diving into turquoise waters, or wandering through timeless cities, Malta offers a little piece of paradise.
